Course Overview

The Certificate in Community Health Assistant – Level 5 is a foundational, competency-based program designed to equip learners with essential knowledge and practical skills for supporting community health initiatives across diverse settings.

The program prepares trainees to work directly with communities, households, and health facilities in promoting preventive healthcare, health education, disease prevention, maternal and child health support, and community-based health interventions.

Learners gain hands-on experience through structured practical sessions and supervised hospital/community attachments, enabling them to confidently support public health programs, community health units, NGOs, and government health departments.

This KHPOA-licensed Level 5 qualification also provides a direct progression pathway to the Diploma in Community Health Assistant – Level 6.

Industrial Attachment

All learners undertake a 3-month hospital or community health attachment in accredited facilities. GoCare provides full placement support to ensure every student gains real-world practical experience.

Career Opportunities

Graduates of the Certificate In Community Health Assistant – Level 5 can work in:

  • Community health programs
  • Government and county health departments
  • NGOs and international development agencies
  • Hospitals and medical centres
  • Public health outreach programs
  • Community health units and CHV programs

Job roles include:

  • Community Health Assistant
  • Community Health Educator
  • Public Health Assistant
  • Community Health Volunteer
  • Maternal & Child Health Worker
  • Health Promotion Assistant
  • Community Outreach Support Worker
